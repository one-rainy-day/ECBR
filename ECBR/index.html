1. Explain the relationship between data, information and knowledge.

  • Data in their simplest form consist of raw alphanumeric values.
  • Information is created when data are processed, organized, or structured to provide context and meaning. Information is essentially processed data.
  • Knowledge is what we know.

2. What is quantitative data and how can you use it.

Quantitative data is data that can be counted or measured in numerical values. It is used when a researcher needs to quantify a problem, and answers questions like “what,” “how many,” and “how often.” This type of data is frequently used in math calculations, algorithms, or statistical analysis.

3. What is qualitative data and how can you use it.

Qualitative data is information that cannot be counted, measured or easily expressed using numbers. It is used to gain insights into people's feelings and thoughts and can be collected using questionnaires, interviews, or observation, and frequently appears in narrative form.

4. Give an example of how quantitative and qualitative data can be used in conjunction with each other

Qualitative data may help researchers to map out survey instruments for use in a quantitative study or researchers can simply use the two types of data together to gain deeper insight into particular questions. For example, to gain information about the quality of life in certain areas, the researchers may refer to quantitative data, such as GDP, literacy rate, longevity rate etc. However, it is also reasonable to interview people living there or receive answers using questionnaires.
